Domains of Interest

Machine Learning

I'm interested in foundational models for scientific applications and agentic artificial intelligence. I really enjoy how mathematics, statistics, and computer science all play import roles in this field. My interest in this field is largely driven by my interest in the subjects that make it up, so I am very open to all the different kinds of ML/AI that are used in industry. Solutions in machine learning have always felt elegant and interesting to me. There's something special about statistics and math at this level and I will always finding inspiring. One of the things I love most about machine learning is that when I read a paper or article, I'm almost always introduced to some technique, method, or idea that is completely new to me. Often, the motivations behind those findings are just as exciting as whatever paper or article I was reading. I'm glad I can appreciate what's special about machine learning and AI outside of all the industry hype.

Algorithms

Algorithms are special in many of the ways machine learning is also unique and interesting to me. Algorithms as a subject is quite broad, but I really do appreciate the mathematical rigor behind every algorithm I've come across. Graph algorithms are particularly interesting since so many problems and ideas can be represented with graphs. State spaces, social connections, and optimal routes are all areas where graph structures naturally expose themselves. Mathematical algorithms, such as gradient descent, are also impressive and grand to me, but I know very few of these algorithms and would like to know more about them as a whole. My experience with the functional programming language OCaml has shown me the brilliance of alternative programming paradigms that might be more suited to algorithmic tasks. When I first started OCaml, our professor showed us what a merge sort implementation looked like in OCaml. Most notably, it was a lot more compact and succint than any other language I had seen. This was really impressive and cool to me, and since then functional programming and it's applications in algorithms have been on my radar.
